Australia's International Trade at a Glance

Visualising key partners, trade balances, and global flow patterns.

Australia's economy is deeply interconnected with the global market, and understanding its international trade dynamics provides valuable insight into the nation's economic resilience, dependencies, and opportunities for growth. This visualisation project — Australia's International Trade at a Glance — explores how Australia engages with its top trading partners, the balance between imports and exports, and the movement of goods and capital across regions.

Trade Flow Map

Origin-destination flows showing Australia's top trading partners. Line width represents trade volume. Toggle between imports and exports to see different perspectives.

Australia's trade is heavily centred around the Asia-Pacific region, with China standing out as the country's largest trading partner by a significant margin. Both imports and exports between the two nations remain consistently high, showing how closely the two economies are connected. Japan, South Korea, and India are also key export destinations, mainly for resources like coal, iron ore, and gas, while the United States plays a major role in imports, particularly for technology and machinery. Overall, these trends highlight how Australia's economy is strongly tied to its regional neighbours, reflecting its role as a major supplier of raw materials and a consumer of manufactured goods.

Exporters by Industry

Number of merchandise exporters across different industries in 2019-20.

This chart shows the number of Australian businesses exporting goods across different industries. The "Other" category represents the largest share of exporters, followed by Wholesale Trade and Manufacturing. Industries like Retail Trade and Construction also contribute, though at smaller scales. This highlights how trade activity extends beyond traditional resource sectors like mining, involving a diverse range of industries. Hover over each industry to view key details about its role in Australia's exports.

Importers by Industry

Number of business importers by industry sector in 2020-21.

The import side shows a similar trend, with "Other" industries again leading, followed by Wholesale Trade and Retail Trade. Manufacturing and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services also play major roles in bringing goods into Australia. This indicates that Australia's imports are spread across both consumer and industrial sectors, reflecting a broad base of economic demand. Hover over each industry to view key details about its role in Australia's imports.
